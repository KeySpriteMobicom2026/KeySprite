Is

#### ⏰ Total Time: ~60–75 minutes  
#### 💡 Learning Goals:
- Practice using scientific language in a real-world context  
- Explore the politics of naming and labeling  
- Build listening and rebuttal skills

### 🔓 Opening Script (5–10 min)  
> “Alright everyone, imagine walking into a supermarket. You see two products: one from a cow, one from a bioreactor. Same taste, same texture, same nutrients. But one says ‘beef,’ the other says ‘lab-grown protein.’  
>
> Does the label matter? Who gets to decide what counts as meat? And why do we even care?  
>
> Today, we’re diving into that question—not just as eaters, but as scientists, lawmakers, and storytellers.”

---

### 🧭 Guiding Questions (to keep things flowing):  
- “Why might a farmer feel threatened by this term?”  
- “Can something still be ‘real’ if it doesn’t come from nature?”  
- “Does changing the name change how people will feel about eating it?”  
- “If we say lab-grown isn’t meat, are we protecting tradition—or slowing progress?”

---

### 🛠️ Group Management Tips  
- Assign roles at the start—let students know they don’t have to believe what they’re arguing  
- Encourage quieter voices: “I haven’t heard from your group yet—what does your stakeholder really care about?”  
- Use time cues: “You’ve got five minutes left to make your point—make it count!”  
- Remind students to use science terms: “Try slipping in ‘cell differentiation’ or ‘biomimicry’—it adds weight to your argument.”

---

### ❗ Common Student Pushbacks & How to Respond  

| Student Says… | Teacher Response |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| “This is pointless—we all know what meat is.” | “That’s exactly what we’re questioning. What if our definition changes over time?” |
| “Why should I care what a farmer thinks?” | “Because food isn’t just chemistry—it’s livelihoods, culture, and identity.” |
| “Lab meat is fake meat.” | “Interesting. What makes something ‘fake’? Is it the process or the result?” |
| “No one’s going to eat this anyway.” | “People once said the same thing about sushi, tofu, and even yogurt. Culture changes.” |

>
> One thing’s for sure—this isn’t just about meat. It’s about who gets to define reality, and who decides what’s normal.”
