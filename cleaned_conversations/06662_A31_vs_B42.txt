## 📜 1. Artist Statement（英文版）  


> The Déjà Vu Archive is an interdisciplinary art project that explores the fragile boundaries between memory, perception, and reality through immersive virtual environments and interactive installations. Drawing from insights in cognitive science, phenomenology, and extended mind theory, this work invites participants to question the reliability of their own familiarity—those fleeting moments when we feel certain we’ve been here before, even if we never have.  
>

Each visitor contributes to a collective memory wall—a temporary archive of personal impressions, drawings, and thoughts—that fades as quickly as it forms, echoing the transient nature of human recollection.  
>   
> The project aims to blur the line between observer and observed, participant and record-keeper, offering a poetic and critical reflection on how our minds construct the world we believe to be real.

---

## 🖼️ 2. Visual Concept Description（可用于提案中的作品说明）

> The installation is designed as a sequential journey:  
> - In the first room, viewers enter a VR environment where visual and auditory cues are subtly delayed or repeated, mimicking the sensation of déjà vu.  
> - Upon exiting, they are invited to contribute to the Interactive Memory Wall, where their input is scanned, processed by AI, and re-projected as part of a shifting collage of shared experiences.  
> - In the final phase, participants wear biometric sensors that track heart rate and breathing patterns. These signals influence ambient lighting and soundscapes in real-time, creating a feedback loop between body, mind, and environment.  
>   
> The entire space is intentionally minimal, with soft gradients, ambient tones, and reflective surfaces that echo the fragility and fluidity of memory itself.
